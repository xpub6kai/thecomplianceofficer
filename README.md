## Hello World

Decided to work this project in public. This is my GitHub repo and I am still learning how to use it.

I am currently developing a digital credit AML program for a bitcoin-collateralised lending business about to establish in Central America. 

I would like to open publish here, an AML programme written from scratch — from first principles, grounded in how bitcoin actually works and what the 
digital credit paradigm actually looks like from a risk perspective.

Not using the legacy regulation headings, not from a compliance firm's template, not from a copy-paste of someone else's legacy template, and not over leaning into the scope creep of become a survielience agent on people financial transactions.

## Why open source an AML programme?

Because the compliance industry has almost no useful open source material for digital-native financial products. What exists is either written for legacy banking, locked behind expensive legal retainers, or so generic it fits nothing. The legacy regime itself is not fit for purpose in a digital era.

Building from first principles took a long time and took a lot of thought about where things are truthfully at, and references case law from multiple jursitications that is being used to to help fintechs navigate the global regulatory terrain. If it saves someone else time, it was worth publishing.

Fintech innovation is reshaping and disrupting traditional banking, payments and asset management industries and challenging long standing models of financial supervision. 

Global innovators are reimagining consumer lending by building products based upon digital credit — a framework that converts digital capital, (ie. bitcoin), into stable financial instruments without the default or counterparty risks of traditional lending models.

Bitcoin backed credit introduces a **collateral first model of lending**. Value is verified through cryptographic proof, secured by energy, and governed by mathematical consensus to ensure provable digital scarcity. In contrast, legacy credit systems rely on a socialised **trust-first model of lending**—trust in institutional judgement, trust in the creditworthiness of borrowers and trust in the stability of the fiat currency that the loan is denominated in.

Underwriters within the traditional, trust based model assess borrowers repayment capacity through extensive documentation: credit scores, income statements, employment records, and appraised asset values. This process relies on centralised intermediaries who collectively maintain confidence in repayment and systemic integrity.

Bitcoin collateralised lending reverses this logic. It treats lending as an asset backed process, native to the digital economy. The borrower’s bitcoin serves as verifiable collateral for a stable instrument (fiat/stablecoin). To mitigate bitcoin’s price volatility, loans are over-collateralised at 150–200%. The money laundering surface is genuinely much smaller than a traditional bank loan and working with immutable digital ledgers makes prescribed AML rules technically redundant. 

An AML programme that reflects the reality of an emerging digital credit ecosytem is worth documenting publicly and openly because it builds upon a sound, and ethically principled foundation. It looks and feels different from a standard VASP template that a cryptocurrency outfit might have in place. There are many concerns about the over extraction of unnessary personal data and of the expolitation of that data for profit.

## What this will become

This repo will grow to include the full AML programme: policy, KYC/CDD/EDD 
procedures, risk matrix, STR framework, jurisdiction risk matrix analysis for a digital native global fintech. It is a living document. It will be wrong in places. PRs and 
issues welcome.

If you are building something similar and want to collaborate or adapt this for 
your jurisdiction, or situation open a discussion.
